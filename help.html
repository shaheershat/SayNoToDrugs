How to Get Help

If you or someone you know is struggling with drug addiction, it's important to seek help as soon as possible. There are various resources available to assist in recovery and offer the support needed during difficult times.

Helpline Contacts

National Helpline for Drug Abuse

Call 1800-11-0031 for free and confidential treatment referral and information on mental and substance use disorders.

How to Approach a Friend in Trouble

If you have a friend or loved one struggling with substance abuse, approaching them with empathy and understanding is the first step in offering support. Here are some tips:

  • Choose the Right Time: Ensure they are in a safe and calm environment before starting a conversation.
  • Be Non-Judgmental: Avoid blaming or criticizing them. Be supportive and show concern for their well-being.
  • Be Patient: Recovery is a process, and it may take time for them to open up or accept help.
  • Offer Help, Not Solutions: Let them know you’re there to help, but don’t push them into decisions they’re not ready to make.
  • Encourage Professional Help: Offer to help them seek professional support or counseling, whether it’s a helpline or rehab center.

Simple Tips for Staying Clean

Staying clean from drugs requires dedication, support, and a positive mindset. Here are some practical tips to help you stay on the path to recovery:

  • Build a Support Network: Surround yourself with friends, family, and professionals who support your recovery journey.
  • Stay Active: Engage in hobbies, exercise, or work to keep yourself busy and focused on positive goals.
  • Set Realistic Goals: Celebrate small victories and set achievable milestones for yourself.
  • Avoid Triggers: Stay away from environments or situations that may encourage relapse, such as parties or bars.
  • Seek Ongoing Help: Continue attending therapy sessions or support groups for long-term success and encouragement.
